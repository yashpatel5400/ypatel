Synalyze

Have you ever had the common office experience of getting dragged into a meeting only to be left feeling like you were wasting your precious time having done so? Synalyze seeks to maximize efficiency for such business meetings

Targeted toward project managers and project leads, Synalze is an app that does natural language processing on audio recordings of business meetings to pinpoint specific points of improvement, such as noting a lack of useful/pertinent participation by members, the emergence of conflict ideas, or the aggressive tones taken by certain members.

The project was developed fully using Python (Watson NLU API) and Ruby on the back-end and standard Bootstrap CSS and JS for front-facing web-app interface.

NeuroBranch

With the saturation of transistors on chips, Intel and AMD have now turned to other means of squeezing out all the performance they can from chips.

The branch predictor is one such component of chips, which does pre-fetching of instructions for the CPU pipeline. In line with the "apply deep learning to everything" mentality that has grown rampant in the tech field, AMD recently applied a very simple neural net to doing this branch prediction.

This project essentially did the same, except by using the gem5 architecture emulator, with a custom C/C++ developed branch predictory. Results are available below.

DeepGIF

Ever wanted to have the joy of applying those cool image style transfer algorithms to GIFs selectively?

Traditionally, image style transfer algorithms have been restricted in two ways: only being applied to still/static images and only with a single "filter." The former has been recently developed by others in applying image style transfer to videos. However, we took it one step further, allowing you to applying different styles for different parts of the video: simply indicate what style you want for which segment of the image in the first still frame, and we'll propagate it through the entire video!

This project was developed in Python (Tensorflow/Keras/OpenCV packages) and JS/CSS for the front-end web app. Repo available below (not currently publicly deployed).

Fluxor

With Elon Musk's announcement of the HyperLoop project, it was pretty difficult to stay uninvoled.

Transportation has become a crucial part of our everyday lives, especially for people not living in cities and commuting from suburban residencies. Even still, it poses a huge problem in the ways of inefficiency, for both the environment and time. Our team, as with many other university teams, wished to participate in this competition, with the intention of improving the state of the matter.

Our team's proposal was developed with the use of passive Maglev suspension in the form of Halbach arrays with a highly streamlined chassis design for minimizing vehicle weight and maximizing cargo space.

Bitcoin/Ethereum: Selfish Mining

With the rise of ICOs, especially on the Etheruem network, focus has to be shifted to ensure that the underlying backbone is secure.

The entire "blockchain" concept is premised on the fact that it is decentralized, meaning that there is no one party or participant that can influence what the history or state of the system is, unlike the traditional client-server architecture, in which the server is all-ruling. Of course, Ethereum was designed with this in mind and uses the traditional proof-of-work method to achieve it.

In this study, we (Professor Matt Weinberg and myself) wished to study an extended claim that an attack called "Selfish Mining" could allow some of the miners to enhance their rewards by witholding the blocks they mined, instead of releasing it, per the Blockchain protocol. Paper available below.

Tesla: Charging Stations

As Tesla and other electric car companies become the new norm, we have to study whether or not the infrastructure surrounding it is sufficient.

Tesla's rise to relevance despite the entrenched automobile giants is an incredible feat. However, their dreams of develop a self-driving electric car that can traverse the country will never be realized without having the necessary surrounding infrastructure, that is, charging stations.

In line with that, we have investigated and compiled a report corresponding to how they should go about optimally placing charging stations. Although our study here was concerned specifically with Chicago, the results and methods are easily extendible to the entire US. Results are available below.
